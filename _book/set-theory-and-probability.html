Advanced Behavioral Statistics

Chapter 2 Set Theory and Probability

In the course of research, behavioral scientists will often take samples of human behavior, e.g.:

  1. A researcher might survey a group of people about their happiness level - with their happiness level characterized on a 10 point scale.

  2. A cognitive researcher may measure how much time a person takes to recall something.

  3. An educational researcher might take a sample of course grades.
